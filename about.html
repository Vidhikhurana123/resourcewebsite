What is Screenprinting?

Screenprinting is a printmaking technique where ink is pressed onto a surface through a mesh screen, which acts as a stencil with areas blocked though an emulsion coating, to create an artwork.

960-1279 AD (Song Dynasty, China)
First known use of screenprinting techniques using silk mesh and stencils for fabric printing.

10th–12th Century (Japan)
Development of more refined stencil and mesh printing techniques.

18th Century (Europe)
Asian printing techniques reach Europe via trade routes, but silk's high cost limits use.

1907 (England)
Samuel Simon patents a screenprinting method for luxury goods using stencils and silk mesh.

1910s–1930s (USA)
Introduction of photo-reactive emulsions revolutionizes the process.

1940s–1980s (India)
Screenprinted matchbox labels with Bold imagery, religious icons, tigers, and pop culture motifs become an iconic part of India's visual culture.
